About Jean-Pierre

Jean-Pierre Caliman heads SMARTRI, the upstream research and development arm of GAR focused on improving agronomic practices.

With 38 years of experience in oil palm cultivation, Jean-Pierre’s expertise extends from soil fertility to the mineral nutrition of oil palms. He is involved in studies related to the agro-physiology of oil palms, aimed at building a mechanistic model of how oil palms to function – from light capture and photosynthesis to the allocation of carbohydrates and yield.

His engagement in the development of ecological intensification of oil palm cultivation has been recognized in Science magazine, which is edited by the American Association for the Advancement of Science, one of the two highest-ranking scientific magazines in the world. He has contributed to the creation of ICOPE, the International Conference on Oil Palm and the Environment, and has been the Chairman of ICOPE since 2010. Jean-Pierre has a Ph.D. in Soil Sciences from the University of Burgundy, France. His thesis was on soil physical fertility in oil palm plantations. He also has a DEA diploma in Agronomy (equivalent to a Master’s Degree) from the Montpellier High School for Agronomy in France, with a specialization in tree crops.

“I believe agriculture and environment are highly compatible and should be integrated, and among all the agricultural crops, oil palm has one of the highest ecological potentials. I am passionate about discovering new technologies and approaches that support the ecological intensification of oil palm cultivation, through enhancing ecosystem services within plantations. This work could help ensure a steady supply of vegetable oil to the people while protecting the environment.”
